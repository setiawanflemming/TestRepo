# 01 Overview

## Problem Statement
Many people set wellness goals but fail to maintain consistency because motivation fluctuates and solo tracking creates weak accountability. Existing tools often optimize for logging, not social reinforcement. This MVP focuses on small, trusted groups where members commit weekly, check in daily, and receive lightweight support.

## Target Users
- Adults pursuing behavior change (fitness, sleep, focus, digital boundaries, routines).
- Users who want accountability without large public communities.
- Users who can commit to short daily updates and weekly planning.
- Users comfortable with small-group interaction (3-4 members).

## Key Principles
### Accountability
- Commitments are explicit and time-bounded (weekly cadence).
- Progress is visible to a small group.
- Daily check-in streaks create urgency and reinforce consistency.
- Weekly summaries close the loop and inform the next cycle.

### Psychological Safety
- Small groups reduce social pressure and noise.
- Interaction patterns prioritize support over judgment.
- Nudges are lightweight and intention-based, not punitive.

### Low Friction
- Daily check-ins should take less than 1 minute.
- Reflections/evidence are optional but encouraged.
- Meetup flow is optional and should not block the core loop.

## Glossary
- **Group**: A small accountability unit of 3-4 users sharing progress.
- **Commitment**: A user’s planned weekly action(s), tracked for completion.
- **Check-in**: A short daily status update tied to a commitment or goal.
- **Streak**: Consecutive days with at least one valid daily check-in.
- **Nudge**: A lightweight peer prompt encouraging action or follow-up.
- **Weekly Summary**: End-of-week aggregation of activity, progress, and trends.
- **Meetup**: Optional in-person or virtual group session proposed by members.
